# Policy Brief

## Title

A federated national surveillance system for medical errors and patient safety in India

## Policy ask

India should establish a federated patient safety surveillance architecture that connects facility reporting, state review, national signal detection, and public learning while remaining interoperable with ABDM and existing quality and surveillance systems.

## Rationale

1. current patient safety learning is fragmented,
2. severe events are inconsistently escalated,
3. national benchmarking and rapid learning are limited,
4. digital public infrastructure now makes structured surveillance feasible.

## Immediate policy actions

1. define a national minimum patient safety dataset,
2. create pilot state safety intelligence cells,
3. notify sentinel-event reporting timelines,
4. issue interoperability and privacy guidance,
5. fund phased implementation in sentinel networks.
