# IJMR submission priority note

## Recommended submission order

1. **First:** federated surveillance framework manuscript  
   File: `ijmr_special_article_surveillance`
2. **Second:** reporting and learning systems review  
   File: `ijmr_review_patient_safety_learning_systems`
3. **Third:** governance and bounded AI manuscript  
   File: `ijmr_governance_bounded_ai_safety_surveillance`

## Why the framework manuscript should go first

It has the strongest India-specific public-health and systems-design identity. It is also the broadest and most foundational paper in the series. For IJMR, that makes it the least niche and the most defensible opening submission.

## Why the review manuscript should go second

It is solid and relevant, but it is conceptually more generic. Its main value is interpretive depth and literature synthesis. That makes it a strong follow-up paper, but not the best first impression if only one manuscript is submitted initially.

## Why the governance/AI manuscript should go third

It is timely and strategically important, but editorially it carries the highest conceptual-risk profile because AI governance can be read as narrower or more speculative unless paired with the broader surveillance framework already established.

## Editorial risk assessment

### Lowest risk

`ijmr_special_article_surveillance`

- strongest India-specific systems framing
- broadest relevance across public health, health systems, and digital health
- least dependent on editorial appetite for AI or implementation theory

### Moderate risk

`ijmr_review_patient_safety_learning_systems`

- strong literature base
- slightly less India-distinct as a standalone submission

### Highest risk

`ijmr_governance_bounded_ai_safety_surveillance`

- timely and strong, but narrower
- more exposed to reviewer concerns about future-facing framing

## Recommendation

Submit the federated surveillance framework manuscript first. If it is well received, use that momentum to submit the reporting-and-learning review next, followed by the governance/AI manuscript.
