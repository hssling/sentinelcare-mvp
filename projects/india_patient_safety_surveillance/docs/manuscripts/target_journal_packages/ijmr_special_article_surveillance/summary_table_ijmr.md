# IJMR summary table package

## Table 1. Core design requirements for a federated patient safety surveillance system in India

| Design layer | Minimum national requirement | Operational rationale | Typical data or workflow elements |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Intake and reporting | Separate daily burden surveillance from event-level case reporting | Prevents overload of routine forms and preserves deeper investigation pathways | Daily denominators, event trigger forms, escalation flags |
| Taxonomy and classification | Use a canonical safety taxonomy across facilities and states | Enables comparability and aggregation rather than narrative fragmentation | Domain, deviation class, process stage, severity, contributing factors |
| Denominators and context | Capture operational denominators and contextual strain indicators | Makes interpretation possible and supports rate-based analytics | Patient-days, admissions, procedures, staffing stress, system downtime |
| Review workflow | Define facility, state, and national review responsibilities | Prevents unowned reports and strengthens learning loops | Triage owner, review deadlines, closure quality, CAPA |
| Signal detection | Support rule-based and pattern-based escalation | Allows early recognition of clusters, recurrent failures, and severe events | Threshold alerts, recurrence checks, high-risk clusters |
| Governance and audit | Preserve traceability, accountability, and policy feedback | Reporting without governance produces fatigue and weak prevention | Audit trail, policy linkage, closure audit, state and national review |
| Interoperability | Align with registries and digital exchange standards | Avoids creation of another siloed reporting portal | Facility identifiers, ABDM-aligned entities, API ingestion |
| AI assistance | Restrict AI to bounded assistive functions under human oversight | Improves structuring and prioritization without delegating accountability | Classification suggestion, summarization, clustering, CAPA drafting |
