# Incidence, Patterns, and Preventability of Medical Errors and Adverse Events in India: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis Protocol

## Registration intention

This protocol is intended for PROSPERO-compatible registration if the review question and methods remain within registration eligibility rules at the time of submission.

## Review objective

To estimate the incidence, distribution, severity, and preventability of medical errors and adverse events in Indian healthcare settings.

## Eligibility criteria

### Population and setting

Patients receiving care in Indian healthcare settings including public and private hospitals, ICUs, emergency care, surgical care, ambulatory settings, maternal and newborn services, and specialty units.

### Study designs

Observational studies, surveillance studies, chart-review studies, trigger-tool studies, incident-report analyses, and prospective or retrospective patient safety studies.

### Outcomes

1. adverse event incidence,
2. preventable adverse event incidence,
3. medication error frequency,
4. diagnostic error or delay frequency,
5. device, procedure, healthcare-associated, or transition-related patient safety event rates,
6. severity and preventability classifications.

## Search strategy

Databases:

1. PubMed,
2. Embase,
3. Scopus,
4. IndMED,
5. Web of Science,
6. Google Scholar for supplementary searches.

Core search blocks:

1. India,
2. patient safety or adverse events or medical errors,
3. medication errors or diagnostic errors or hospital-acquired harm or incident reporting,
4. incidence or prevalence or surveillance or epidemiology.

## Study selection and extraction

Two independent reviewers will screen titles and abstracts, review full texts, and extract data using a standardized form. Disagreements will be resolved by consensus or third-reviewer adjudication.

## Risk of bias and synthesis

Study quality will be assessed using design-appropriate tools. Random-effects meta-analysis will be conducted where outcome definitions and denominators are sufficiently comparable. Otherwise, structured narrative synthesis will be used.

## Reporting

The review will follow PRISMA 2020 and relevant methodological guidance for prevalence and incidence syntheses.
