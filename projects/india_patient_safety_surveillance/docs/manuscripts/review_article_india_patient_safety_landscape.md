# Patient Safety Reporting and Surveillance in India: Current Landscape, Gaps, and Design Implications for a National System

## Abstract

India has multiple quality, vigilance, surveillance, and digital-health initiatives, but these do not yet amount to a coherent national patient safety surveillance system. This narrative review examines the current landscape, identifies major gaps, and derives design implications for a future national patient safety intelligence architecture.

## Current landscape

The present landscape includes hospital quality committees, accreditation-linked incident review, pharmacovigilance, hemovigilance, local root-cause analysis practices, selective digital incident systems in larger hospitals, and broader digital public-health infrastructure. These elements create partial capacity but not a unified surveillance chain.

## Core gaps

1. inconsistent event taxonomy,
2. under-reporting and variable safety culture,
3. weak cross-facility aggregation,
4. limited state and national signal intelligence,
5. low integration between reporting and corrective-action tracking,
6. limited publication and benchmarking outputs.

## Design implications

A national system should be federated, non-punitive, integration-ready, and centered on a minimum dataset plus explainable signal workflows. It should not depend on a single software vendor or a single operational model.
