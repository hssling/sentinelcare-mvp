# Governance, Ethics, and Federated Learning Design for a National Patient Safety Surveillance System in India

## Core argument

A national patient safety surveillance platform will fail if governance, privacy, and protected learning are treated as downstream policy issues instead of operating system requirements.

## Governance requirements

1. protected reporting pathways,
2. role-based review,
3. clear separation of surveillance and punitive action,
4. audit trails,
5. state and national scientific oversight,
6. publication governance and privacy review.

## Ethics stance

The system should prioritize patient protection, workforce learning, fairness across states and sectors, and prevention of misuse of de-identified or aggregated data.

## Federated learning model

The learning model is federated in both data and governance terms: local cases remain locally actionable, while higher tiers aggregate signals, generate guidance, and steward taxonomy and policy.
