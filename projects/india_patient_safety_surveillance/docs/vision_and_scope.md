# Vision and Scope

## Vision

To build a federated, explainable, governance-first surveillance system for medical errors and patient safety in India that can support routine reporting, near-real-time signal detection, structured investigation, accountable corrective action, and national learning.

## Problem definition

India has multiple health programs, quality initiatives, and disease surveillance systems, but patient safety surveillance remains fragmented. Safety events are often managed locally, reported inconsistently, classified non-uniformly, and weakly linked to investigation outcomes, policy feedback, or national situational awareness.

## Strategic goals

1. create a minimum national data model for patient safety events,
2. enable low-burden reporting from public and private facilities,
3. support state and district patient safety review cells,
4. detect recurring hazards across geography, specialty, device, medicine, and process type,
5. connect local corrective action to national learning,
6. support publication-grade analytics and public accountability without compromising patient privacy.

## Design principles

1. federated by default,
2. human-governed,
3. integration-ready for ABDM and existing health information systems,
4. non-punitive learning orientation with protected investigation pathways,
5. structured enough for analytics but simple enough for routine use,
6. scalable from sentinel pilot networks to national roll-out.
